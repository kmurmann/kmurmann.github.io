About

Katia Murmann is a Managing Editor at Swiss Blick Group, focusing on digital projects, long forms and storytelling. Previously Katia had a career with several big media companies in Switzerland, including SonntagsZeitung and Schweiz am Sonntag. She studied Orientalism and Politics at the Universities of Munich and Tehran and graduated from Deutsche Journalistenschule in Munich (40. Lehrredaktion).

In her work she focusses on digital transformation, the future of journalism and the evolving craft of storytelling. Katia invests heavily in the education of the new generation of journalists. She is jury member of Dr. Georg Schreiber Medienpreis, who aims to support young journalists in-depth reporting on social issues.

Projects

In March and April 2016 Katia mastered her digital skills at UC Berkeleys Advanced Media Institute. Her main project focussed on the changes the news-industry is going through. She looked at it with the eyes of a man who has been delivering the San Francisco Chronicle and other newspapers for more than 50 years. Eric Smith wonders: Will he outlast print or will print outlast him? At the SF Chronicle Katia talked to Kristin Go, Managing Editor of SF gate.com. She explaines where the papers sees its digital future.

Work

For SonntagsBlick, the biggest Sunday paper in Switzerland, Katia and her colleagues have realized several Digital News Packages. The last one, Rätselmorde, concentrated on unsolved murders. The series initiated a broad public discussion about the fact, that murder in Switzerland cannot be persued after 30 years. As a result several members of parliament aim to change this fact. .
